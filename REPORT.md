## 1. Project Overview
The "BLOCK-O-MICS" project is an end-to-end bioinformatics pipeline engineered to analyze how the gut microbiome influences host gene expression during ageing. We specifically investigated whether the presence of bacteria (Conventional) vs. their absence (Germ-Free) causes greater transcriptomic shifts than the process of getting older.

## 2. Technical Architecture
The system is built on a modular "Unix-philosophy" design, separating concerns into specialized components:

*   **Workflow Orchestration:** Managed by **Snakemake**. This ensures that data fetching, processing, and statistical modeling happen in the correct order and are 100% reproducible.
*   **Data Ingestion (`src/io.py`):** Automatically retrieves raw count matrices from NCBI GEO and simulates metadata based on the experimental design of study GSE278548.
*   **Bio-Processing (`src/processing.py`):** Performs CPM (Counts Per Million) normalization and Log1p transformation. It utilizes **scikit-bio** for high-dimensional ordination (Bray-Curtis PCoA).
*   **Statistical Modeling (`src/stats.py`):** Implements **PyDESeq2**, the industry standard for differential expression. It includes a custom mapping layer to translate Ensembl Gene IDs into human-readable Gene Symbols.
*   **Visualization (`src/viz.py`):** Generates publication-quality (300 DPI) scientific plots with optimized contrast for clinical presentation.
